## Launch Strategy: Staged City Rollout

Don't launch everywhere at once. Seed one city densely, make it feel alive, then expand.

**City #1: Łódź**
- Home turf — Mateusz can seed manually, ask personal network
- Target: ~20 motivated early contributors to cover city centre stations
- Aim for ~1,500 MAU before expanding to city #2
- Use rack-derived estimated price ranges (Story 2.11) to fill gaps before community reaches critical mass

**City #2+: Deliberate ignition**
Each new city needs a small ignition event — don't rely on organic spill alone (it can happen but it's not a strategy). Repeat the Łódź playbook: seed data, local outreach, local micro-influencer if budget allows.

**Expansion trigger:** when Łódź station coverage feels reliably fresh (most stations updated within 7 days) — that's the signal the playbook works and it's time to push city #2.

## Influencer / Creator Outreach

### Priority targets

| Creator | Why | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Złomnik** | Irreverent no-BS personality, "not getting ripped off" is on-brand, loves underdog stories | 500k-1M+ subs. Probably 5-15k PLN rate card but may do it for the story |
| **Motobieda** | Frugality and value is his whole brand — perfect fit | Similar tier |
| **Motodoradca** | More mainstream, wider reach | Good for scale after proof of concept |

### Approach
- **Don't lead with money.** Lead with the product and the story.
- Cold outreach is fine — worst case is no.
- Follow and engage genuinely for a few weeks before DM-ing.
- Pitch structure:
  1. One paragraph: who you are, what you built, why (solo founder + AI team + real problem)
  2. One paragraph: why you thought of *them specifically* — be genuine
  3. One ask: early access, no strings, no obligation to post
  4. Zero pressure, zero rate card in first message

### Timing
Send outreach when:
- App is live with real data in at least one city
- Product feels polished enough to use without friction
- You have a concrete story: "X drivers in Łódź saved Y PLN in the first month"

Too early = "looks interesting, I'll check back" — and later never comes.

### Why the story works for these creators
- Solo founder built with AI — genuinely interesting angle
- No corporate agenda, no VC money
- Solving something Poles feel viscerally — fuel prices are a national obsession
- "Help your viewers not get ripped off at the pump" — easy content hook

## Content & SEO Strategy

**Core insight:** "ceny paliwa" search intent is high-volume and high-commercial-intent in Poland. Owning those keywords drives free user acquisition.

### Content types

**Automated / data-driven (publish weekly):**
- Regional fuel price reports: "Ceny paliwa w Polsce — tydzień X" — generated from platform data, charts included
- Voivodeship comparisons: "Gdzie jest najtańsze paliwo w Polsce?"
- Price movement alerts: "Ceny benzyny rosną — co mówią dane rynkowe?"

**Editorial / evergreen:**
- Eco-driving guides (fuel saving tips)
- "How fuel prices are set in Poland" explainer
- ORLEN rack price explainer — what it is, why it matters to drivers
- Seasonal pieces: "Why fuel is more expensive in summer"

**Data journalism (higher effort, higher reward):**
- Monthly Poland fuel price index — citable by press
- "Which Polish city overpays most for fuel" — inherently shareable, drives installs in every city mentioned
- Year-on-year comparisons once data accumulates

### Distribution
- Publish to web portal (Epic 10.1) — SEO primary channel
- Seed weekly reports into relevant Facebook groups (Polish motoring, personal finance, city-specific groups)
- Twitter/X thread format for price movement stories

### Website ads
- Google AdSense or similar on web portal
- Expected: $20-50/mo early, $200-500/mo at meaningful SEO traffic
- Doesn't compromise app trust — different surface, different context
